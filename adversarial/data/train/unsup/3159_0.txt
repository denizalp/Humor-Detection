Executive summary, ranked best to worst:\n\n1. Paris - Le Village Buffet  (Frenchy, but superb and succulent)\n2. Rio - Carnival World Buffet (great overall, but off-strip)\n3. Planet Hollywood - Spice Market Buffet (varied experience, but usually good)\n4. Harrah's - Flavors Buffet (suprisingly good, but suffers from its surroundings)\n5. Caesars - Lago Buffet (frustrating . . . a few easy changes could make this so much better)\n6. Flamingo - Paradise Garden Buffet (meh . . . but look! there's a chocolate fountain!)\n7. Imperial Palace - Emperor's Buffet (it's just kind of sad)\n\nOverall summary:\nIts a pretty decent value for $45, there are at least 3 decent buffets in there. \n\nNotes on our strategy for eating 2-3 plates at 7 different buffets in a 24 hour period:\n* We planned for starting the 24-hour clock in the late morning, so we could go to the last buffet the next day in the early AM\n* We researched the buffets beforehand, and sandwiched the ones we knew wouldn't be very good between the good ones\n* Lots of walking up and down the strip in between buffets was beneficial\n\n24 hours, 7 buffets, about 3 plates per buffet ends up being about 16,000 calories total, or 666 calories per hour, or about 356 calories per dollar. I call that cost effective.\nI should say somewhere that I don't normally engage in this kind of wanton gluttony, but I do enjoy a challenge.\n\nNone of the 7 buffets were anywhere near as good as the Wynn, Bellagio or Wicked spoon, IMHO.  But there are at least 3 good buffets in there so its a pretty good deal for $45. \n\nPictures of my plates:\nParis
